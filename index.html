The scale of unemployment that AI has the potential to cause is so immense it's hard to really understand what the big blob of green covering your screen really means.
Humans have a hard time understanding the scale of really big numbers, so this website hopes to help you understand just how many people would be out of a job.
Every 10 pixels you scroll is 5000 people.
Let's put these unemployed people in perspective by comparing it to some familiar things.
Population of California.
Population of the United States of America.

AI is currently projected to put 4.5 Americas out of work within the next 10 years.

If you were to pick a person randomly out of everyone on Earth, you'd have a 1/5 chance of choosing someone who wants to work but can't find a job.

Furthermore, not everyone is at the same risk of losing their jobs to AI.

The US House of Representatives claims that 40% more women work in occupations at risk for automation than men.

On top of that, Black and Latino Americans are at much higher risk of job and wage losses.

The US House of Representatives insists that they're making a plan on how to deal with these projected unemployment rates, but there's nothing available as to what they have planned.

I think it goes without saying, but if AI has the potential to cause such wide-spread unemployment issues, something needs to be done to make sure that companies are working with the best interest of mankind at heart, rather than pure profit.

Profit isn't worth putting nearly a quarter of the World's population out of a job.

Humanity is at the mercy of their own greed now, and we have to rely on governments to keep us safe from technology replacing us in all jobs, with restrictions on AI, training programs to prepare ourselves for the changing job market, and unemployment benefits to help when millions inevitably find themselves jobless.
